FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

How do I start a chapter?

Fill out a short application form. That’s it!

What are the membership fees?

Membership is completely free! Starting this year, we will not be charging students to participate in their school clubs or participate in our Stock Trading Competition.

What can financial literacy do for me?

Although financial education is not a required component of the school curriculum, it is one of the most important skills to have as a student. It is crucial in helping you understand key aspects of business, find a job, save for your retirement, or even to support yourself independently after graduating from high school. You will gain invaluable skills with Target Alpha that will be undoubtedly be used in any career that you wish to pursue.

Will we trade stocks?

By participating in Target Alpha by either joining a school chapter, or by registering individually, you will have the opportunity to participate in this year’s National Stock Trading Competition. The competition will be held on Investopedia’s stock simulator from November 7th, 2016 to February 20th, 2017. For more information including registration details, please visit our events page.

What if my school doesn’t have a Target Alpha club? Can I still join?

If there is not yet a Target Alpha chapter at your school, we strongly encourage you to start one on your own! Many of our chapters are new this year, and we will always provide support to help you get your club started. We provide all Target Alpha chapters with a school start-up package, an exclusive curriculum to help you guide your lesson plans, and any other guidance you may need.

Can single individuals compete?

Of course! You do not need to be a part of a Target Alpha chapter to join our events. Both the National Stock Trading Competition as well as the Stock Pitch Competition can be done individually, but you can also choose to compete in a team of 2-3 members.

Why the name 'Target Alpha'?

The word “Alpha” is a commonly used word in finance to describe returns that exceed the general market. “Target” can be viewed as a goal that one sets out to achieve. When combining the two, we can translate the two words into, "Our goal is to achieve above average returns."
